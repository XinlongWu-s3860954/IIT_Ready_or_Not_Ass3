Project Idea

Smart fridge software -- ReadyDiet

Ready or not’s Project idea is a software that allows users not only to track their daily diet intakes and what they consist of in a detailed manner, but it also provides an advanced storage and identification system where every product is scanned through a barcode scanner which identifies its contents such as what it is made out of and its expiry date.

This software will be called ReadyDiet

Overview



   Ready or not aims to provide the world with a easy software that helps with the exhausting and sometimes expensive dietary needs as well as those long shopping lists that are always forgotten. This software will change peoples lives and how they operate in the food and diet industry.

   This project consists of a software that is designed to scan barcodes on items and tell you exactly how much calories, kilojoules, ingredients and everything else that is in the food. The software can also keep track of all food that you have eaten, all food you have bought and all food you have stored away. Another cool feature of this software is the recipe lookup, it checks to see what foods are in the inventory and scores the web for recipes that can be made with what’s available This software is a sort of food organizing, monitoring and developing software which helps users watch their calorie intake, cook healthy meals and know what they are missing in their fridges or storages.

   This 3-step software is all about Preparing finding and knowing which foods or products to use, buy or eat when cooking storing or locating foods / products.

Motivation

  • Ready or not’s motivations consist of eradicating the expensive and extra time wasted creating accurate diet plans and knowing which exact food to buy and store.
  • Why ruin the fun of making healthy food and enjoying it by paying for experts and going through time-wasting processes just to give up on the whole idea?
  • ReadyDiet is going to drastically bring down healthy eating to the simplest form whilst ultimately providing easier ways to keep track of our food products.
  • These real-world issues show our great interest in solving these problems using our software.


   This project will help millions if not billions of people. Everyone wants to eat healthy and be the perfect weight and make sure they are getting enough nutrients, however not everyone has the time to figure out a plan or has the money to afford a nutritional expert to guide them. Just opening this application and adding your food items that you eat throughout the day is enough to tell you if you are going over your limits or not, making it 10x easier than doing the research and paying a professional. Furthermore, keeping track of the items, you buy and what you are missing for your plans helps you organize and possibly save money on certain food aspects. This software will be the go-to application to figure out a 5-star meal plan and ingredient sorter.s

Competitive Products

   There are already apps that help with dietary needs and organizing fridges and pantries such as Fridge Pal, fridge check, calorie counter app, Lite n’ easy. These are some of many of our competitors in our current market however they do not and will not provide the services and extra utilities that come with our ReadyDiet software. We gain a long advantage by combining both the likes of a dietary application and a food product organizing application both into one software for all your food product needs. Another large point of difference is that many of these applications require confusing and sometimes annoying process not to mention the costs to keep these applications running as well.

Aims

   The core aim of the project is to develop a tool which is found in most households domestically, essentially the creation of a barcode scanner integrated into a fridge. The product monitors the consumption experienced by the user, and in turn allows the user to monitor the amount of calories they are consuming, which is especially useful for those engaged in physical exercise and demand an accurate measurement of calories consumed. In turn, the long-term aim is to see this product marketed not only to a small group of physical exercise enthusiasts but instead to the entire domestic market as a whole, to ensure that the general public maintain high health standards for themselves.

Finding the tools to create the project

   The first goal to enforce the idea, is to find the tools needed to create such a product. Through finding what is required to bring the project to life, the team can then go ahead and develop the technology and find suitable interfaces which make the use of the product easiest for the consumer. When the right tools are provided, then the project can be pursued at the most efficient rate, which can lower the cost and time involved with the creation of the project.

Ascertaining the target market / demographic

   The second goal for the project is to find the demographic which the product is targeted towards. Through finding the target market, the team can come to a conclusion whether the product is financially viable upon completion, and determine if there is enough demand for the product at the end. Through finding out the market, the product itself can be marketed towards those most likely to buy it, and in turn raise revenue. Therefore, finding the core demographic is key to the success of the product.

Creating the software required

   Through creating the software, the team is able to test ‘loosely’ if the concept will work prior to the integration of the scanner into a physical fridge. This goal must be reached before the project can progress, as without cleaning up the software side of things, the physical prototype success will be unattainable. Therefore, the software must be complete, for the project to enter its next phase of development.

Creating physical prototypes

   The creation of physical prototypes is imperative to the success of the project, and will be completed upon the creation of the software, which will aim to make user interface easy. The physical prototype will be in the form of creation of the barcode scanner first, which will provide details of the food scanned, and show the calories and nutritional statistics of the scanned food. The final form of the prototype will be implementation into a fridge interface, as the product must be integrated without appearing too ‘clunky’ or unappealing. Therefore, the success of this goal is important to the project in its entirety.

Implementation into the market

   The project will be complete once all patent and trademarks are attained, and the product can now be shown to major electronics brands, and marketed to be integrated into their existing products, such as Hisense and Samsung. Through achieving the implementation of the product into a widespread major brand, the product can achieve its end goal of the lifespan and return finances to the project owners. The market implementation will be difficult, but once achieved, mark the end of the project, and the success of it at the same time.

Testing

   As I am the lead software designer and developer, I have taken responsibility to create mockups as shown of the software on a phone application and how it would look like.

  The first stages of the mockups, from ReadyDiet beta 1.0 - 1.2 are being tested by only the developers including me. The reason being that this is only the first design stage and will only need to create ideas and designs on this stage.

  The rest of the ReadyDiet Beta stages will be tested by at least 5-10 different families. The families will have sizes including families with 3 members to families with 12 members all together. This is to test the app's limits and how much data and how fast it can process and save all diet information on a family. Some issues such as the application slowing down due to the number of data being inputted or it can crash. The families will also be of different backgrounds and cultures. This is to test whether the application can pick up diverse ranges of foods in our databases. These tests will also help us fill in any missing items into our database.

  We will find these users to test our application by asking randoms on the street if they are interested with the benefit of having a free diet monitoring application to use early. If the users get really hard to find, we will work with research recruitment agencies to fulfill our tests. Ofcourse the software will be limited to its features however more beta roll outs will continue and more tests will be done to improve our application.

Plans and Progress

   There are roughly three kinds of major outputs from our project. Software should be our primary output, which includes, but is not limited to, customer-side applications in the user's personal device (e.g. smartphone/smartwatch). There is also business-side software used by production merchants/manufacturers. In order to enable the software to read the required information from the QR code/barcode, we also plan to offer a public protocol specification for storing the information that we need in a specific format. Because it is a public protocol specification, it can be easily reused by other products, thus further helping us to create a commercial ecosystem. In order to promote our software more widely so that it can reach more embedded devices (e.g. refrigerators). We may also need to experiment with hardware products that allow embedded devices to install or interact with our software.

   For the sake of time, we have only planned the most major programs so far. In this development cycle, we are planning to implement a user application for IOS first, which will be called ReadyDiet. After that, we should also need to build the corresponding back-end services for ReadyDiet. So far, we have discussed and identified some basic information about our application. For example what features it will contains. The colour style of its theme. The development language and the technology stack etc.

   We discussed the features that should be included in our application, firstly the core base function of our application - the scan code, which should be extremely basic and essential. Then we needed to parse and store the information that was scanned. And as mentioned in the project plan, the information will be analysed to remind the user.

   We initially planned to use Python for our application development as it was simple enough and would save us a lot of time. But we learnt that python is not suitable for developing IOS applications. So we had to use Swift to develop the app for IOS, which fortunately was just as simple. In the meantime, we plan to use python to develop future back-end services. But in the end we discovered that python has a Kivy library that allows us to develop IOS applications using python. So we finally returned to our initial decision to develop the whole project in python.

   We also considered whether to use Jira or Trello for project management, but after careful discussion we felt that although it was necessary, we could not use it yet, at least at the beginning of the project. We plan to use Trello to manage the project when it is well underway.

   We are currently working on a prototype of the application - what it will looks like and how it responds to the user's actions. Almost all of us had no experience in prototyping, so to simplify our work, we chose proto.io instead of figma. proto.io is simpler and allows us to drag and drop the corresponding components directly to design

   We are all well aware that this is a huge project and if we need to hand over the project to another team at the end of this term. Then the most important thing is that we need to make sure the other team knows what our project is about, our project plan and the current progress of our project. Therefore, as can be expected, we will have many synchronous handover meetings. During the meeting , we will explain our project goals, starting from how we came up with the idea for the project, to how it finally takes shapes. Next we will try to explain our project plan and the current progress of this project. In the meantime, we will gradually hand over the resources we have for this project during this process, such as the github repository, the proto.io organisation and related documentation, etc. Then we will give the new team some time to work independently on our project, during this time, they are allowed to ask us any questions they met about the project to ensure a smooth handover.

Group processes and communications

In this project, we used Teams as the main team communication tool. Basically all communication takes place in teams. We basically meet twice a week, the first time on Wednesday, which is an offline meeting where we synchronise each of us in the classroom with our individual progress from the previous week and work together for two hours. Then a second meeting is booked for the week, which is usually on a Friday afternoon.

In the second meeting on Friday afternoon we will summarise our work for the week and then plan everyone's tasks for the following week. This is a short meeting and we will record the meeting for review purposes. In week 13, both meetings became online meetings. As Assignment 3 closes at the end of the week, our Wednesday meeting briefly summarised the final work we needed to do for Assignment 3. And there will be one final meeting at the end of the week.

During the course of the meetings, we did have people who were not able to attend because they had other commitments. This is not avoided and our current solution is to skip the absentees for the time being. Then use Teams to synchronise progress and assign tasks with the absent person via messages.
